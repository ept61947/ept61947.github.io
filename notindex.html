The ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba) is a medium-sized tree native to the Orient, but widely planted in the Southestern US. The ginkgo is considered a “living fossil,” in that there are no closely related species still extant. Ginkgos are prized for their vivid yellow fall leaves.

An unusual biological feature of the ginkgo is that it is a gymnosperm (“naked seed”), meaning that the ginkgo seed is not encased in a fruit.
